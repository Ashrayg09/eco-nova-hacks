What is it

About the event

Eco Nova Hacks is a weekend hackathon for students who care about the environment. You'll form a team, pick a problem worth solving, and build a real sustainability project in 24 hours, then show it to judges on Sunday. Bring a laptop and an idea; we'll handle the food, space, and mentors.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

No experience needed

Workshops run all weekend and mentors are around whenever you're stuck. Plenty of people here are building their first project.

🛠️

Build what you want

Apps, hardware, data tools, research, or policy work all count, as long as it ties back to sustainability.

🎓

Meet people

Talks from people working in climate and clean tech, plus time to actually meet other students between builds.

🏆

Win prizes

Cash and gear for the strongest projects, with separate prizes for beginners and standout design.

Good to know

FAQ

Who can attend?

Any current high-school or college student, regardless of major or experience. First-timers are especially welcome.

How much does it cost?

Nothing. Eco Nova Hacks is 100% free, including meals, snacks, and swag, thanks to our sponsors.

Do I need a team?

Nope. Come solo or with friends. We'll run a team-formation session at the start so no one hacks alone.

What should I bring?

Your laptop and charger, a student ID, and anything you need to stay comfy overnight (a hoodie, toiletries, maybe a sleeping bag).

Do I need to know how to code?

Not necessarily. We welcome designers, researchers, and idea people. Workshops and mentors will help you learn as you build.

Is it in person?

Yes. The entire event runs in person at the Jacobs School of Engineering at UC San Diego.
